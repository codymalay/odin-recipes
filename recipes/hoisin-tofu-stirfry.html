Hoisin Tofu Stirfry

Home

Description

This sauce is a homemade recipe that came to fruition after finding a recipe that I wanted to try, but I realized I didn't have the ingredients I needed for the sauce! So, I slapped together the substitutes I had in the fridge and viola, new recipe achieved! This is best served over steamed rice, but I like using quinoa as well. Sweet, spicy and most importantly, tasty!

Ingredients

  • 1 block extra firm tofu, pressed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into chunks
  • 1-2 shallots, sliced
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb ginger, minced
  • 3 tbsp hoisin
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1-2 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • Red pepper flakes for desired spice level
  • Green onions, chopped
  • Option to add any other veggies you have on hand

Steps

  1. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and chop tofu into bite size pieces
  2. Add good amount of oil and fry tofu. You need enough oil for a shallow fry. If frying in batches, add oil between each batch. Place tofu on a wire rack when golden brown on all sides
  3. While tofu is cooking, mix sauce ingredientss until well blended - hoisin, soy sauce, gochujang, white wine vinegar, mirin, and red pepper flakes
  4. Once tofu is done, remove nonstick skillet and add wok to the burner over medium-high heat with a drizzle of oil
  5. Add peppers and shallots and cook 3-4 minutes, stirring often tto prevent burning
  6. Add ginger and garlic, stirring constantly for 30 seconds or until fragrant
  7. Add sauce and tofu to the pan. Toss to coat tofu and cook a few more minutes until sauce thickens
  8. Serve over rice, and garnish with green onions. I also like to layer a handful of spinach between the rice and tofu, and sprinkle everything with sesame seeds
