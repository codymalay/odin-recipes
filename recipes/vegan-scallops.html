Vegan Scallops

Description

This recipe for vegan "scallops" is easy, affordable, and surprisingly similar to actual scallops!

You can find King Oyster mushrooms at your local Asian market. Mine sells packages of 6 or 7

for under $3. We served these as an appetizer to the main course, but they really stole the show!

Ingredients

  • 3 king oyster mushrooms (sometimes called trumpet mushrooms)
  • 2-3 tbsp unsalted vegan butter
  • 3-4 sprigs of thyme
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • salt & pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat a cast iron skillet over medium to medium-high heat.

  2. Slice the mushrooms into scallop-sized rounds. I shoot for 1-1.5". Trim the end and caps,

    and throw away any woody ends. Keep the edible parts and slice them, we'll still cook these!

    Score each side of the mushrooms, this will add surface area to allow the butter to soak in.

  3. Add a neutral oil to the skillet. Once hot, add your scallops and sear, about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Flip and add butter, garlic, sprigs of thyme, and any leftover sliced caps/stems. Let the butter melt,

    then butter baste the scallops to keep them moist and ensure they are cooking all the way through.

  5. Once they are golden brown on both sides, remove to a plate and sprinkle with salt and pepper.

    Optional: garnish with fresh thyme leaves.
